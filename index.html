Why is this important?





The restaurant industry is essential to New York Cityâ€™s social and economic fabric. More than 60 percent of NYC resident restaurant workers were immigrants in 2018. 44% of the workers were Hispanic and 20% were Asian. By August 2020, employment in the restaurant industry was still only 55 percent of its level in before the pandemic.



According to Womply's data, Chinese restaurants are being hit the hardest. The study showed that 55% of small and local businesses owners reported anxiety over business survival if sales stop for 1-3 months and restaurant businesses are less likely to survive disruptions. The study also looked at the disruptions experienced by restaurants with offerings that are commonly associated with take-out, such as fast food, Chinese, Thai, and Indian cuisine.



The study analyzed credit card transaction data in restaurants between January 1 and March 1, 2020. They identified a restaurant as closed if the restaurant did not have any transaction for at least three days in a row. Furthermore, they also categorized restaurants that are fully operating via food delivery services under 'closed', since the transaction is being taken care of by a third party.



However, another metrics showed a different result. According to Safegraph , Chinese restaurants are experiencing decrease in foot traffic just as bad as Mexican, Italian, Korean, and Japanese restaurants. However, this data seems to be highly impacted by the shelter-in-place regulation that many American cities employed during the early days of the pandemic. Since the research analyzed foot traffic to businesses and restaurants, the reduced number might be driven by the fact that people are self-quarantining in this period of time.
